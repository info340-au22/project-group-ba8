Events Map

Problem Overview

Joining events could be an exciting thing to many people. We are social animals and we need various events to meet friends or intake some joyful air. However, planning events can be problematic. You and your friends decided to have dinner at Juan's Restaurant, but it turns out that there're 3 Juan's Restaurants in town. You planned to go to a concert after visiting your uncle, but it turns out that there's no direct public transit between the two places which you expect would only take a 20-minute bus ride. You occasionally got a discounted weekend trip to Hawaii, but it turns out that all travel guidelines online extends 4-day long at minimum.

We had too much headache when managing events neatly and clearly. There are plenty solutions on the market, but non of them provides an integrated experience from the start to the end of an event. We have Microsoft ToDo, a todo list sharable with friends, but it's good only for brief tasks instead of comprehensive events. We have various social apps, where we can share plans and links with each other, but we need to jump back and forth between apps if you have multiple links. We also have Tripadvisor which has a robust ecosystem for travel management, but it falls short if you made your own events. These platforms also have strong risks of fake content that deceives users, said by Forbes. Moreover, one can cost a lot of time traversing through different platforms to collect and write down information of an event, or a poorly informed event could cost time of the attendees. Integrating the experience into one platform could be a life-saver.

App Description

Our app will create an event map for students who struggles to arrange their events and estimate the time they run from one event to another.

Designated Functions

  • import a map of any place
  • freely add a description of the place
  • upload photos of it to change the "background" of one spot on the map
  • set up an alarm clock to remind them of their business hours
  • update what they wrote and keep multiple version of a place's description
